-> In CSS, a pseudo-class is a keyword added to a selector that styles an element based on its state or position in the document, rather than its class or ID. -> A pseudo class always starts with a single semi-colon(:). Examples of pseudo classes : 1) :first-child - Targets the first child. 2) :nth-child(3) - Targets the 3rd child. 3) :nth-child(even) or :nth-child(odd) - Targets all the even/odd numbered elements. 4) :link , :visited - Targets the plain link and visited states of the links. 5) :hover, :active - Targets the hover and active states of the links.
